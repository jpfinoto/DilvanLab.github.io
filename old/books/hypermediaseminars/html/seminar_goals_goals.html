1. Goals

Research the state-of-the-art relating the multimedia and hypermedia technologies with other computer technologies, such as computer networks, data banks, distributed systems and information visualization.

This course gives the students the opportunity to research in detail the field in which they will be doing their graduate work in relation to other fields in computing. The course will work mainly with presentations of papers from multimedia and hypermedia conferences and magazines. The presentations can be done by the teacher, students or, whenever possible, invited experts.

Topics of interest include: Multimedia Data banks, Multimedia Protocols, Multimedia Operating Systems, Multimedia Distributed Systems,Multimedia Interfaces, Systems to Support Multimedia Collaborative Work, Multimedia Data Storage and Retrieval, Visualization of Hypermedia and Multimedia Information, Hypermedia and Multimedia Document Modeling and Authoring, Automatic Authoring of Hypertext Structures, Hypertext Engineering, Multimedia Documents Evaluation, Hypertext Structures Evaluation, Multimedia and Hypermedia Applications in Education, Software Engineering, Cooperative Work, among others.
