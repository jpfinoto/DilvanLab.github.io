1. Course Methodology

This course is centered in four goals: the four experiments the students have to perform. Each experiment will have a group of students in charge of it. This group will give a lecture with theoretical material covering the needs of the experiment, make a test about this theoretical material, prepare extra material for the experimental class (like questions for the reports), conduct the experiments with the other students and grade the tests and reports made by the other students.

The tests and reports will be reviewed by the course monitor.

There will be 3 classes for each experiment. Each student should read the recommended texts BEFORE each experiment's first class. The 3 classes will be divided in:

  1. Circuit theory about the experiment and a test.

  2. Experimental class for the first half of the students.

  3. Experimental class for the second half of the students.

After the last experimental class the students will have one week to hand in an individual report about the experiment. This report should be in the HTML format (the Word editor can save in this format) and handed in using the electronic tools provided.

The groups in charge of each experiment should upload the material for the theoretical class before the day of the class. This material should be in the HTML format.

FINAL GRADE

Each student final grade will be the average of:

  1. His group's grade.

  2. The average of his tests grades.

  3. The average of his report grades.
